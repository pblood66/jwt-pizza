# Incident: 2026-04-05 12-30-00

## Summary

Between 12:30 and 12:50 on April 5, 2026, 12 active users experienced a complete inability to place pizza orders. The event was triggered by a chaos monkey injection from the pizza factory at 12:30. The chaos monkey caused the factory to reject all incoming order requests and return an error payload instead of fulfilling them, resulting in 100% of order attempts failing. The event was detected by a Grafana alert on pizza creation failures. Patrick Blood responded, identified the factory report URL in the error response body, and visited it to end the chaos event. This high-severity incident affected 100% of users who attempted to order during the 20-minute window.


## Detection

This incident was detected when the pizza creation failure alert was triggered in Grafana and Patrick Blood was paged.

To improve time-to-detection, the Grafana alert threshold could be lowered so that even a small spike in failures triggers a page sooner, reducing the window before investigation begins.

## Impact

For 20 minutes between 12:30 UTC and 12:50 UTC on April 5, 2026, all pizza order creation requests failed. 

This incident affected all 12 active users (100% of active users during the window), who experienced complete inability to place pizza orders. 

No support tickets or social media posts were submitted.

## Response

After receiving the Grafana alert at approximately 18:37 UTC, Patrick Blood came online and began investigating. 

He examined the factory error responses, located the reportUrl embedded in the JSON payload, and visited it to end the chaos event. John Doe confirmed orders were succeeding again and declared the incident resolved at 18:50 UTC. No escalation was necessary.

## Root cause

The pizza factory injected a chaos monkey event that caused it to reject all incoming order requests and return an error payload in place of a fulfilled order. Because all order creation passes through the factory, 100% of orders failed for the duration of the event.

## Resolution

Service was restored by visiting the reportUrl provided in the factory's error response body:

https://pizza-factory.cs329.click/api/support/8c0ecb303714425eb25be9c9461ff390/report/ls0axypi

This signaled to the factory that the chaos event had been acknowledged, after which it resumed fulfilling orders normally.

To improve time-to-mitigation, the service could automatically extract and prominently log the reportUrl from factory error responses. For example as a Grafana annotation or Slack notification so the on-call engineer can act on it immediately without manually inspecting raw response bodies.

## Prevention

This was the first recorded chaos monkey incident. No prior incidents share this root cause.

Steps have been made to increase the website's sensitivity to chaos monkeys and new logging strategies will be implemented to reduce downtime.

## Action items

1. Lower the Grafana alert threshold for pizza creation failures to reduce time-to-detection
2. Add explicit logging of the factory reportUrl whenever a factory error response is received, surfaced in a prominent location such as a Slack alert or Grafana annotation
